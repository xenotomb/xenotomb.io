Music

The music for Xenotomb was originally inspired by Amiga era module chiptunes, made by Neo Te-Aika. The native format is extremely small in file size, can loop (easier than typical audio formats) from points other than the start of the song, and is perfectly playable in most modern audio players, as well as GZDoom.

We has changed to using MIDI however, because it can be more versatile, can sound different across many different devices, and overall is easier to work with and higher quality. It's good to provide options, so we make sure the music sounds good in OPL, Roland based MIDI synths (virtual and real Sound Canvas), and a variety of popular soundfonts.

An SC-55 recorded arrangement soundtrack will be released with the 1.0 release of the game, however you can check back to find more tracks under the below playlist as they're completed.
